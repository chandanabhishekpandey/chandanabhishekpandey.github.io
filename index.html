Hi, I’m Chandan

I completed my Ph.D. in Spintronics at the Fert Beijing Research Institute under the supervision of Professor Weisheng Zhao. Currently, I am driving research at CIC nanoGUNE targeting light-matter interactions, magneto-thermo-plasmonics, and advanced nanomagnetic systems.

EXPERIENCE

Postdoctoral Researcher

Experimental physicist at CIC nanoGUNE, San Sebastián, working on magneto-thermo-plasmonics, light–matter interaction, and artificial spin-ice systems. Focus on nanoscale heat-assisted magnetic phenomena and their application to emergent quantum and topological states.

Nanofabrication & Laboratory In-Charge

Responsible for operation, maintenance, and user training of nanofabrication facilities. Hands-on expertise in e-beam lithography, AJA sputtering systems, thin-film deposition, and process optimization for spintronic and nanomagnetic devices.

Spintronics & Magnetic Nanodevices

Extensive experience in CoFeB/MgO-based MTJs, antiferromagnetic materials, and nanoscale magnetic characterization using SQUID-VSM, nanoMOKE, and magneto-optical techniques.

Doctoral Researcher (PhD)

PhD in Microelectronics and Solid-State Electronics, focused on spintronic devices and magnetic tunnel junctions. Research combined device fabrication, magnetic characterization, and theoretical understanding of nanoscale phenomena.

Research Highlights

Magneto-Thermo-Plasmonics

Postdoctoral research on plasmon-assisted photothermal heating in magnetic multilayers and nanostructures, with applications to artificial spin-ice systems and nanoscale control of magnetic states.

CoFeB/MgO Spintronic Devices
