About Me

I am an experimental physicist specializing in nanoscale magnetism and spintronic devices. My work integrates nanofabrication, interface engineering, and microscopic characterization of magnetic heterostructures, including MgO-based magnetic tunnel junctions, with a focus on voltage-controlled perpendicular magnetic anisotropy, spin–orbit torque switching, and terahertz dynamics.

In addition, I have explored artificial spin ice systems, emphasizing nanoscale magnetic behavior and light–matter interactions. I have led collaborations with diverse research groups, trained bachelor’s and master’s students in thin-film deposition and nanofabrication, and combine experimental expertise with computational modeling using LABVIEW, MATLAB, Python, COMSOL, MuMax3, and VASP to study emergent phenomena in functional nanomaterials.
